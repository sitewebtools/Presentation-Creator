# You’re Probably Using PPT Templates Wrong

This document summarizes a practical, no‑fluff guide to understanding what PowerPoint templates are actually for — and how to pick one that doesn't make your life harder. The original article was written by **Yongbin Cheung**.

> **Original article:** [You’re Probably Using PPT Templates Wrong](https://yongbincheung.substack.com/p/youre-probably-using-ppt-templates)

The article cuts through the common misconception that templates are mostly aesthetic choices. Instead, it explains how a template is a **formatting system** — and why most free templates fail not on looks, but on structure.

---

## What a Template Is (And Isn’t)

A PowerPoint template (.potx file) stores your slide master, layout hierarchy, color palette, fonts, and default formatting. When you open it, all of that scaffolding is already in place — every new slide inherits those settings automatically.

- **A badly built template** — even a beautiful one — will cost you hours: locked elements, placeholders that move when you type, colors that break when you copy slides.
- **A well-built template** makes working in PowerPoint feel effortless: everything snaps into place, consistency happens automatically.

> A template isn’t a pretty background you paste your content onto. It’s a formatting system.

---

## The Three Things That Actually Make a Template Useful

Most guides focus on visual style. That’s the least important dimension. Here’s what actually matters:

### 1. Slide variety
How many different layout types are built in? A template with only a title slide and a generic content layout forces you to build everything else from scratch. The best templates have 15–25 layouts covering common content types. Check `View > Slide Master` before committing.

### 2. Placeholder behavior
Placeholders should resize and reflow content sensibly. If a heading placeholder can only hold two short words before the font size drops to unreadable, that’s a structural problem no amount of visual polish can fix.

### 3. Font and color portability
If you share files with people on different computers, non‑standard fonts will substitute. Safe templates use fonts that ship with Windows and Mac by default. Color palettes should use the theme color system properly, so changes apply across the whole deck.

These three things have nothing to do with whether the template looks modern — they determine whether it’s actually workable.

## Where to Find Templates That Are Built Correctly

Most free template sites prioritise design over structure. A few sources are reliably worth checking:

- **For business and professional presentations:** Slidesgo – carefully maintained free library (account required).
- **For minimal designs with solid fundamentals:** SlidesCarnival – clean, well‑structured, no account needed.
- **For AI‑generated templates with content built in:** AiPPT – pick a design from [AiPPT’s template library](https://www.aippt.com/templates) and the tool generates actual slide content from your topic, notes, or uploaded document. Covers business, education, medical, marketing, sales, and more.
- **For specific diagram and chart types:** PresentationGO – charts, timelines, process diagrams, comparison layouts.
- **For guaranteed compatibility:** Microsoft’s built‑in library (File > New) – reliable, uses system fonts, zero compatibility issues.

## The Template vs. Theme Confusion

In PowerPoint, “template” and “theme” are different things:

- **Theme (.thmx)** controls colors, fonts, effects – the visual layer.
- **Template (.potx)** includes the theme plus slide masters, layouts, and potentially pre‑built content.

When you apply a theme to an existing presentation, you change how it looks. When you start from a template, you’re starting with a structural framework that includes the design. This matters because it affects how you work.

---

## When a Template Isn’t What You Need

Templates solve a specific problem: consistent formatting across a multi‑slide deck. They’re less useful or counterproductive in some situations:

- **One‑off, 5‑slide internal deck** – blank file with manual formatting might be faster.
- **Company branded template exists** – use it, even if you don’t love the design. Consistency has real value.
- **Your content doesn’t fit the template’s structural assumptions** – match the template to the content type, not just the industry.

## The Download Trap

A significant amount of time spent searching for free PPT templates is wasted on sites that show previews of things you can’t download without upgrading.

**Reliably free sources:**
- Slidesgo (with account)
- SlidesCarnival (no account)
- PresentationGO
- SlidesMania (education‑focused)
- AiPPT’s template library ([https://www.aippt.com/templates](https://www.aippt.com/templates)) – free tier
- Microsoft’s built‑in options (free with PowerPoint)

Everything else is worth checking the download conditions before you spend time browsing.
