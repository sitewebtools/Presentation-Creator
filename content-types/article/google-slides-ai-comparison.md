## The Problem

Google Slides is free, collaborative, and cloud‑based, but its built‑in AI (Gemini) is still limited. Dedicated AI presentation tools either work inside Slides as add‑ons or generate full decks that must be exported. Choosing the right tool depends on whether you must stay **inside** Google Slides during the whole process, or simply end up with a working Google Slides file.

---

## The Testing Setup

All tools were evaluated with the same business prompt:

> *“Create a 10‑slide presentation for a SaaS product that helps small businesses automate their invoicing. Include a problem statement, solution overview, key features, pricing, and a call to action.”*

The four evaluation criteria were:

1. Quality of the first draft  
2. Amount of editing required  
3. How well the output works in Google Slides  
4. Real‑world usability for client decks

## A Question Most People Overlook

> *Does your audience actually open the source file?*

Many teams and individual presenters obsess over staying in Google Slides, but in reality most audiences only see the presentation as a PDF, a shared link, or a live screen. If the answer is **“no”**, then the best tool is the one that produces the highest quality first draft – even if it means a quick export/import step.

- **When you must stay inside Google Slides** (heavy collaboration, strict IT policies) → **Plus AI** is the strongest add‑on.  
- **When you simply need a great deck that works in Google Slides** → **AiPPT** gives the best quality and fastest first draft. The import takes two minutes.
