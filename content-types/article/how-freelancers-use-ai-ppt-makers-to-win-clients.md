## What to Look for in an AI Presentation Tool

- **Control over output parameters** – slide count, tone, audience, language.
- **Multi‑source import** – accept PDFs, Word files, URLs, and handle 10,000–15,000 words.
- **Model flexibility** – choose faster models for drafts or more sophisticated models for final pitches.
- **Template variety** – filtered by industry and style, so you don’t fight the design.
- **Language accuracy** – handles multiple languages without breaking layouts.

## Actual Workflow: Brief to Final Pitch (45–90 minutes)

1. **Gather everything** – client brief, background materials, call notes, case studies, testimonials.
2. **Set parameters** – slide count (e.g., 12–15 for a 20‑min meeting), tone (corporate vs startup), audience.
3. **Generate initial structure** – upload materials, let AI build content flow and apply a design template.
4. **Fix the narrative** – reorder slides, cut what doesn’t serve the pitch, adjust language to match the client’s business.
5. **Replace generic visuals** – swap stock photos for project screenshots, client logos, or custom‑generated images.
6. **Add proof points strategically** – place case studies and testimonials *after* the problem and solution.
7. **Final check** – typos, brand consistency, working links, read aloud.

**Manual building used to take 5–6 hours. AI workflow takes 45–90 minutes.**

## Mistakes That Kill Your Pitch

- **Trusting AI to understand your client** – always customise.
- **Ignoring your brand** – align output with your colours, fonts, and style.
- **Letting AI dictate the story** – ensure a clear arc: problem → solution → proof → next steps.
- **Using placeholder visuals** – generic images make you look generic.
- **Forgetting the call to action** – always end with a specific next step.
