## What Makes a Good Presentation Website?

A strong presentation website should offer:

- **Clear data visualisations** – charts, graphs, and infographics that make complex information easy to understand.
- **Sharing and collaboration** – real‑time editing, commenting, and version history for teams.
- **Highly customisable templates** – editable PowerPoint themes that align with brand identity.
- **Strong transitions and animations** – smooth effects that keep audiences engaged.

## Key Highlights from the Original Article

### 1. Canva
- **Best for:** Highly customisable design, large template library, multimedia support.
- **Pros:** Easy drag‑and‑drop, brand kit, real‑time collaboration.
- **Cons:** Premium features require Pro plan; slower with media‑heavy slides.

### 2. Prezi
- **Best for:** Zoomable, non‑linear storytelling – ideal for pitches and education.
- **Pros:** Engaging transitions, cloud‑based, export to PDF/PPT.
- **Cons:** Learning curve; free plan restricts templates and storage.

### 3. AiPPT.com
- **Best for:** AI‑powered slide generation from text or documents, no watermark.
- **Pros:** Instant outlines, AI image generator, 200k+ templates, export to PPT/PDF.
- **Cons:** Limited offline functionality; best results depend on input quality.

### 4. Visme
- **Best for:** Data‑rich presentations with charts, infographics, and interactivity.
- **Pros:** Advanced analytics, brand management, export to PPT/PDF/HTML5.
- **Cons:** Steeper learning curve; advanced features require paid plan.

### 5. Powtoon
- **Best for:** Animated and video‑style presentations.
- **Pros:** Customisable scene transitions, cloud‑based, branding tools.
- **Cons:** Free plan includes watermark; learning curve for complex animations.

### 6. Pitch
- **Best for:** Team collaboration, modern interface, analytics.
- **Pros:** Real‑time co‑editing, version history, slide libraries.
- **Cons:** Fewer multimedia/AI features; less suited for highly creative decks.

### 7. Beautiful.ai
- **Best for:** Automated slide formatting and design consistency.
- **Pros:** Smart templates, AI‑powered layout, team commenting.
- **Cons:** Limited flexibility for custom designs; premium required for advanced features.

### 8. Zoho Show
- **Best for:** Seamless integration with Zoho ecosystem and third‑party apps.
- **Pros:** Cloud‑based, real‑time collaboration, export to PPT/PDF/HTML.
- **Cons:** Limited animations; best for users already in Zoho environment.

### 9. Gamma
- **Best for:** AI‑generated slide decks and web‑native presentations.
- **Pros:** Responsive format, multimedia support, interactive elements.
- **Cons:** Heavy reliance on AI limits customisation; free plan includes Gamma branding.

### 10. Emaze
- **Best for:** 3D and HTML5 presentations with immersive design.
- **Pros:** Ready‑made structures, automatic resizing, version tracking.
- **Cons:** Free plan includes Emaze branding; advanced features in higher tiers.

### 11. Haiku Deck
- **Best for:** Minimalistic, storytelling‑focused presentations.
- **Pros:** Clean design, high‑quality image library, export to PPT/PDF.
- **Cons:** Limited customisation; best for short, visual‑heavy decks.

### 12. Slides.com
- **Best for:** Developer‑friendly customisation using HTML/CSS/JavaScript.
- **Pros:** AI‑powered content generation, offline presentation support.
- **Cons:** Free presentations are public; editing requires internet.

### 13. Google Slides
- **Best for:** Free, cloud‑based collaboration across teams and devices.
- **Pros:** Real‑time editing, add‑ons, offline mode, live Q&A.
- **Cons:** Less visual customisation compared to premium tools.

### 14. Mentimeter
- **Best for:** Live audience interaction (polls, quizzes, word clouds).
- **Pros:** Real‑time feedback, analytics, pre‑designed templates.
- **Cons:** Free plan limits questions per presentation; fewer animation options.

### 15. Publuu
- **Best for:** Converting PDFs into interactive flipbook presentations.
- **Pros:** Embed videos/links, viewer analytics, ideal for marketing.
- **Cons:** Not optimised for traditional slide decks; limited offline.
