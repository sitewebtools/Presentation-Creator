## How to Choose Good Presentation Topics

Have you got a presentation coming up soon? Check out a few practical tips to help you choose the right topic:

✅ **Audience relevance:** Any topic you go for should be one that your audience is familiar with and can easily relate to.

✅ **Personal interest:** Select topics that you enjoy talking about frequently and also suit your experience level.

✅ **Clarity:** Go for clear topics, rather than those with extremely broad or vague themes.

✅ **Engagement potential:** Lastly, choose topics that can provoke interest or discussion so your audience remains attentive.

### 1. What is a 5-minute topic for a speech?

Short speeches work best when the topic is simple, relatable, and easy to explain within a limited timeframe. A good 5-minute topic should allow the speaker to share clear points without rushing.

A strong 5-minute speech topic could be “The impact of social media on daily life.” It’s broad enough to discuss opinions and examples, yet focused enough to cover in just a few minutes.

### 2. What is the 777 rule for presentations?

Presentation rules help speakers create slides that look clean, engaging, and easy for audiences to follow. The 777 rule is one of the simplest guidelines used to avoid clutter and information overload.

The 777 rule suggests using no more than 7 lines per slide, 7 words per line, and keeping each slide visible for about 7 seconds. It encourages clarity, stronger visuals, and better audience attention during a presentation.

### 3. What are the best general topics?

General presentation topics work well when they’re broad, relatable, and easy for any audience to understand. They don’t require specialized knowledge, making them suitable for school, work, or casual presentations.

Some of the best general topics include:

1. The importance of time management
2. How technology shapes daily life
3. Benefits of lifelong learning
4. The power of positive habits
5. Why communication skills matter

These themes provide flexible and engaging talking points.
